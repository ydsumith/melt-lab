Multiscale Engineering Lab for Thermofluids

Simulation-driven discovery in thermofluids, interfaces, and biomedical systems.

The MELT Laboratory develops computational models that connect molecular-scale physics, nanoscale heat transfer, interfacial transport, and biomedical mechanics.

Research Vision

At the MELT Lab, we advance computational research at the intersection of molecular dynamics, nanoscale heat transfer, thermofluids, and biomedical engineering.

Molecular Dynamics

Atomistic and coarse-grained simulations for water, interfaces, evaporation, and biomolecular systems.

Thermofluids

Multiscale modeling of heat transfer, evaporation, pressure distributions, and interfacial transport.

Biomedical Mechanics

Computational studies of fibrin, thrombosis, clot mechanics, and biomolecular assembly.

AI-Driven Modeling

Machine learning and optimization methods for force-field development and simulation analysis.

Research Areas

Current and past projects span water force-field development, nanoscale evaporation, thrombus mechanics, fibrin polymerization, and local thermodynamic property estimation.

Force Field Development for Water

Development of coarse-grained and atomistic water models for evaporation and interfacial transport.

Nanoscale Heat Transfer

Molecular simulations of liquid–solid interfaces, high heat flux removal, and passive-flow cooling.

Thrombus and Fibrin Mechanics

Reactive coarse-grained models of fibrinogen polymerization, clot formation, and clot deformation.

Local Pressure Estimation

Efficient post-processing algorithms for density, pressure, surface tension, and local continuum properties.
