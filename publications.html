Note on Open Access

Every day, countless scientists and researchers, myself included, contribute to the growing body of knowledge by submitting our research work, both funded and non-funded, in the form of papers. Ironically, while the authors of these crucial discoveries do not receive any form of royalty, the majority of these papers are only accessible through costly subscriptions, placing them out of reach for many.

This practice, which began in the late 1990s, has created a somewhat exclusive domain, often reserving knowledge for those with the financial means. However, the advent of open-access journals and peer-reviewed preprints has started to challenge this norm, providing a more inclusive platform for sharing research.
